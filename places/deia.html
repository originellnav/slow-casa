Deià, and the particular silence of the Tramuntana

The mountain village on Mallorca's northwest coast where artists have been coming to disappear for a century — and where it is still possible to do exactly that.

The road into Deià drops through terraced olive groves older than any building in the village. There are no chains here, no branded anything. Just stone, sea light, and the sound of water moving somewhere below the road.

Robert Graves arrived in 1929 and never really left. The poet and novelist found in Deià something that has since become harder to locate in Europe: a place that resists being consumed. The village sits 400 metres above the sea in the Serra de Tramuntana, surrounded by mountains that block the north wind and face the water directly south.

The village

Deià has perhaps 700 permanent residents. The main street takes four minutes to walk end to end. There are two good restaurants, a bar, a small market, and a church at the top of the hill where Graves is buried. That is essentially everything, and it is enough.

Getting there

Deià is 30 kilometres from Palma along the Ma-10 mountain road. The drive takes around 45 minutes and is one of the finest roads on the island. A car is essential.

What to do

Swimming at Cala Deià, the rocky cove 20 minutes on foot down the mountain. Eating at Es Racó d'es Teix on a warm evening. Walking the old stone path to Sóller. Reading on a terrace until the light goes.
